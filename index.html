Getting here

Travel

Flying

The best way to get to Sibiu International Airport (SBZ) from Central, South and Western Europe (or anywhere else in the world) is flying with a layover in Germany (Munich) or Austria (Vienna). Other options include also flying to Cluj International Airport (CLJ) or to Bucharest Otopeni Airport (OTP).


  Zurich - Sibiu (SBZ)

  Geneva - Sibiu (SBZ)

  Basel - Cluj-Napoca (CLJ) +   ~2 hours to Sibiu.


If flying to Bucharest (OTP) is the better option, one can get to Sibiu either by driving (recommended) or by train:


  Bucuresti - Sibiu (~4 hours)

  Bucuresti - Sibiu


Do not forget to adjust the arrival and departure times as best suits you for the above directions.

Staying here

Accommodation

Both Sibiu and Sibiel offer wonderful places to stay. Sibiel has a charming agrotourism scene and holds the distinction of being the first village in Romania to embrace it – pretty impressive, right?

However, if you're seeking a livelier atmosphere, Sibiu might be more your speed. You'll find plenty of cozy Airbnbs in the Old Town and Main Square, as well as larger hotels like Continental and Hilton, along with some charming boutique hotels scattered around the city center.

No need to worry about transportation during the wedding festivities – we've got you covered with arrangements between Sibiu and Sibiel. Of course, if you prefer the convenience of Uber, that's always an option too!

Feel free to reach out if you have any questions before making your reservations. Below, we've included some recommendations to help you in your decision-making process:

Hunedoara

  • Wake up late, enjoy some coffee and lunch in Timişoara.
  • Optional city guided tour.
  • Drive to Hunedoara.
  • Visit Corvinilor's Medieval Castle on the way to the national park.
  • Dinner and Sleep in Hunedoara in some nice hotel.

Retezat National Park

Close to where Andrei comes from lies the most beautiful mountain range in Romania.

  • Option 1) Hike to Retezat Peak and Bucura Lake.
  • Option 2) Visit Sarmisegetusa ruins
  • Sleep in a mountain hut or mountain airbnb cabin.

Sibiu Medieval Town

One of the nicest medieval towns in Romania with a lot of Habsburg influence.

  • Hike down from Retezat or do a shorter nature walk in the national park.
  • Drive to Sibiu.
  • Visit the city.
  • Sleep in a nice bohemian hotel near Sibiu.

Central Carpathian Mountains

Rich both in natural beauty and history, this part of the country has much to offer.

  • Drive on the famous TopGear Transfagarasean mountain pass roads.
  • Reach Balea Lake.
  • Hike or enjoy activities at the lake. Maybe you get to see a bear :P
  • Enjoy some fresh Romanian white cheese and shepherds traditional recipes.
  • Sleep in the mountain cabin or in an airbnb cabin.

Brasov and Peles Castle

A mix of the medieval town of Brasov and the bourgeois style of royal Peles Castle.

  • Drive to Brasov and visit the old town.
  • Enjoy lunch in the traditional restaurants Brasov
  • There might be some music festivals going on in Brasov.
  • Visit Peles Castle ( much nicer than Dracula's castle).
  • Sleep in a mountain airbnb.

Bucharest

Leaving the Carpathian mountain range to reach the vibrant Romanian capital of Bucharest.

  • Drive to Bucharest.
  • Visit the city and enjoy the busy style of Bucharest after the tranquil medieval towns.
  • Visit the Parliament house which is the third-largest administrative building in the world after the Pentagon.
  • Enjoy the nightlife in Bucharest.
  • Fly directly from Bucharest the next day or drive back to Timişoara.
